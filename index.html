Object Passport: The Atom

  • Core Components Proton (+): Positive charge, defines the element (Atomic Number).
    Neutron (0): No charge, adds mass (glue).
    Electron (-): Negative charge, orbits in shells.
  • Key Terms Atomic Number (Z) = Number of Protons.
    Mass Number (A) = Protons + Neutrons.
    Isotope = Same protons, different neutrons (e.g., C-12 vs C-14).

Sci-Pop: We are Stardust

Hydrogen and Helium were created in the Big Bang. Every other element in your body—the Calcium in your bones, the Iron in your blood—was forged in the explosive death of stars (supernovae) billions of years ago. You are literally made of star debris.

Structure & Properties

How to Read the Map

Groups (Columns) Elements have the same number of valence electrons. Behave like a family (similar reactions).
Periods (Rows) Elements have the same number of electron shells.

The "Families"

  • Group 1: Alkali Metals 1 valence e-. Highly reactive. Explode in water. (Li, Na, K)
  • Group 17: Halogens 7 valence e-. Reactive non-metals. Love to steal 1 electron. (F, Cl)
  • Group 18: Noble Gases 8 valence e- (Full shell). Snobs. Do not react. (He, Ne, Ar)

NaCl Ionic Bond (Table Salt)
Ionic Bond (The Thief) Metal gives electron to Non-metal. Creates charged ions (+ and -) that attract.
Example: NaCl, MgO
Covalent Bond (The Sharer) Two non-metals share electrons to fill shells.
Example: H2O, CO2

Sci-Pop: The Rarest Element

Francium (Group 1) is the rarest natural element. Scientists estimate there is less than 30 grams of it in the entire Earth's crust at any moment. If you dropped a chunk of it in water, the explosion would be catastrophic—but you can't gather enough to try!
